The Cliffs of Moher

Step on to the edge of the world and into an awe-inspiring view that dreams are made of – at the Cliffs of Moher you will encounter nature in its wildest, purest form – see the rugged cliffs facing the mighty ocean, taste the salt air, hear the birds cry, feel the ancient rocks beneath your feet, smell the wind. Decide today to grant your highest wish to visit Ireland's most spectacular natural wonder at the heart of the Wild Atlantic Way – the Cliffs of Moher Visitor Experience.

Whether it be the cliffs themselves carved by time, ocean and weather into fantastic shapes and formations, the mighty Atlantic ocean or the birds, plants and other wildlife that inhabit the cliffs and the sea there are plenty of natural wonders to enjoy here. The Cliffs of Moher are officially part of the world's top 14 Natural Wonders.

The Cliffs of Moher are sea cliffs located at the southwestern edge of the Burren region in County Clare, Ireland. They run for about 14 kilometres. At their southern end, they rise 120 metres (390 ft) above the Atlantic Ocean at Hag's Head, and stretch eight kilometres to the north, reach their maximum height of 214 metres (702 ft) just north of O'Brien's Tower, a round stone tower near the midpoint of the cliffs, built in 1835 by Sir Cornelius O'Brien, then continue at lower heights.

Cliffs of Moher Visitor Center

The award winning eco-friendly centre has been built nestled into the hillside and features a restaurant, cafe's, visitor information and gift shops. The unmissable cliffs exhibition animates the story of the Cliffs, presenting the geology, wildlife and human aspects in an entertaining and informative way.

The Cliffs of Moher Visitor Experience is open all year round with the exception of Christmas Eve, Christmas Day and St. Stephen's Day. Opening times vary between 9.00 am 9.00 pm. During the summer, closing time varies with sunset times. For example the Cliffs are open until 9pm for the months of July and August. Why not choose to visit later in the day during the summer when the Cliffs are less crowded and enjoy a magical sunset, great photo opportunities or a romantic picnic?

The cliffs of Moher visitor center contains a range of videos and scripture explaining anything to do with the cliffs of moher. Whether it be information about the surrounding birds and animals (such as the seagulls) or information about the local flora, the indoor visitor center has got it all covered in great detail!

For more information, visit www.cliffsofmoher.ie or telephone 065 708 6141. Admission price includes entry to the Cliffs, Visitor Centre and Parking. Adults 6 euro, Seniors 4 euro, Students 4 euro and children under 16 are free.
